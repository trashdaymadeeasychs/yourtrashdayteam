# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Charleston-area homeowners, short-term rental owners, business owners, and property managers who are tired of dealing with trash day. They land here from a referral, a truck decal, a door hanger, or a social post, usually on a phone, often in the evening. Their job to be done: decide in about ninety seconds whether these two local crews are legitimate, understand exactly what $70/month buys, and hand over a card without anxiety. A secondary audience is property managers evaluating the service for multiple units, who skim for "licensed and insured," pricing clarity, and cancellation terms.

## Product Purpose

Your Trash Day Team is a co-branded offer from two established Charleston companies: Trash Day Made Easy (weekly bin roll-out and roll-in valet) and The Bin Boy (monthly bin washing). The page is the entire product surface: one landing page that explains the combined service, earns trust fast, and converts through a Stripe card-on-file signup form (no charge today, Pending Approval, auto-billed $70 on the 1st after approval). Success is a completed signup form, or a phone call to 843-955-3132 from someone who almost signed up.

## Brand Personality

Hardworking, neighborly, spotless. This is a crew you'd wave at from the porch: friendly and plain-spoken, but visibly professional (licensed, insured, five-star reviewed). The voice says exactly what the service costs and does, with zero SaaS gloss and zero eco-guilt messaging. Emotional target: relief ("one less chore, handled by people who show up") plus confidence ("my card is safe, I can cancel anytime").

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient meshes, hero metrics, icon-card grids, startup vocabulary ("supercharge your trash day").
- National franchise waste-industry sites: clip-art trucks, red-white-and-blue urgency banners, popup coupon spam.
- Eco-brand cosplay: leaf icons, "green planet" messaging, recycled-paper textures. The service is about convenience and cleanliness, not environmental virtue.
- Anything that hides the price or the billing terms. The candor IS the brand.

## Design Principles

1. **The price is the hero.** $70/month, what it includes, when billing happens, and how to cancel appear early, plainly, and more than once. Clarity converts better than persuasion here.
2. **Trust before transaction.** Real logos, a real phone number, licensed/insured/reviewed proof, and honest billing language must all be visible before the card fields.
3. **Two crews, one team.** The co-brand is the story. Both identities stay visible and equal; the page ties them together rather than inventing a third brand that erases them.
4. **Blue-collar craft, not startup polish.** The page should feel like a well-kept work truck: sturdy type, confident color, everything labeled, nothing decorative for its own sake.
5. **Never break the form.** The Stripe signup flow is the business. Every visual decision defers to form legibility, error clarity, and completion on a phone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Audience skews broad (homeowners of all ages), so: minimum 16px body on the form, visible focus states on every control, error messages tied to fields, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected, color never the only signal, and touch targets at least 44px on mobile. The page must remain fully usable if Stripe fails to load (clear fallback to the phone number).
